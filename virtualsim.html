VirtualSIM

An AI-based educational platform that trains emergency medicine residents through simulated cases.

The problem

The hardest moments in emergency medicine — breaking bad news, disclosing a medical error, running a cardiac arrest — are exactly the ones residents get the fewest chances to practice. Real patients are not practice, and traditional simulation labs are expensive to staff and hard to schedule. Most residents simply learn these moments by living them.

What VirtualSim does

VirtualSim lets residents rehearse on simulated patients who talk back. A resident picks a scenario — a difficult conversation, a chest-pain workup, a code — and the case unfolds in real time: the patient responds emotionally to what the resident actually says, vital signs change with each decision, and the story branches accordingly. When it ends, the resident gets an immediate debrief — feedback on empathy, clarity, and clinical choices — while educators can review session recordings and performance trends across the program.

The scenario library spans difficult conversations, clinical cases, resuscitation practice with a live cardiac rhythm display, and a multi-patient “running board” that simulates managing several patients at once — the defining skill of emergency medicine.

Where it stands

VirtualSim is live and in active development, in use alongside the Elevate residency platform.
