ED Operations Data Models

A suite of data models built to optimize emergency-department operations.

The problem

Emergency departments generate mountains of data, but almost none of it arrives in a form a leader can act on. The questions that decide how a department runs — does staffing match when patients actually arrive, where do patients wait longest, who leaves before care is complete — go unanswered, not because the data doesn't exist, but because nobody has shaped it into an answer.

These models were built inside a large hospital system to close that gap. Each follows the same philosophy: start from minimal raw inputs — often just a handful of timestamps — and end in a simple dashboard a leader can read at a glance.

The models

  • P2P — Patient to Provider. Four timestamps become the three phases of every visit — arriving, being treated, departing — and a picture of department load by hour.
  • R2D2 — Resources to Demand. Patients arriving per hour against the staff on hand, showing exactly when coverage and demand fall out of step.
  • LBCC — Left Before Completion of Care. Who walks out before care is finished — when, at what acuity, and with what complaint.
  • C3 — Chief Complaint Categorization. Thousands of free-text visit reasons sorted into meaningful buckets for flow and utilization analysis.
  • TTM — Time to Manage. How imaging decisions ripple into a patient's length of stay.
  • VeLO — Velocity. Predictive modeling from historical patterns: what volume and acuity a given shift is likely to bring, so staffing can anticipate rather than react.
  • Bamboo. Understanding the department's most frequent returning patients.
  • MSR — My Shift Report. A web dashboard for recording key shift events in real time — the model that grew into its own platform.

Around the models sits a workbook system — locked metric definitions, a leadership dashboard, and site-level report cards — so every number means the same thing to everyone, every month.

Where it stands

The models are in regular leadership use, and their locked definitions now form the data contract for the next generation of this work: an AI-enabled ED operations dashboard with conversational drill-down, currently in pilot.
